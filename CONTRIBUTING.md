# Contributing a Lens

Want to run a song through the Music Lens? Here's how to submit one.

---

## Before You Start

Read [FRAMEWORK.md](FRAMEWORK.md). The lens has rules. If your output interprets lyrics, judges the artist, moralizes, or gives advice, it doesn't belong here. The lens describes what the music does emotionally inside the listener. That's it.

---

## How to Submit

1. Fork this repo
2. Create a new file in the `lenses/` directory
3. Name it: `song-title-artist.md` (lowercase, hyphens, no special characters)
   - Example: `comfortably-numb-pink-floyd.md`
4. Follow the template below exactly
5. Open a pull request with the song title as the PR title

---

## Template

Copy this and fill it in. Do not add sections. Do not remove sections. Do not rename sections.

```markdown
# [Song Title] — [Artist]

**[Track number if applicable] / [Duration] / [Album], [Year]**

---

## What this song feels like

[Immediate emotional atmosphere. What does the air feel like when this song is playing? Describe the sensation, not the content.]

## Core emotional movement

**[Movement] → [Movement]**

[How does the feeling change over the course of the song? Use the primary movements: Stillness, Build, Drift, Loop, Hold, Break, Return, Rise. You can chain them. Describe what happens inside the listener as the song progresses.]

## What it's really about (emotionally)

[The underlying emotional state being expressed. Not the narrative. Not the lyrics. Not the artist's intention. What is the internal experience? What state does this song put you in?]

## How the music mirrors it

[How does the sound itself reflect the emotional state? Talk about tempo, space, density, repetition, dynamics, instrumentation, production. What is the music doing that matches what the listener feels?]

## Emotional center

[One or two sentences. The stable emotional truth of the piece. The thing that stays after the song ends.]

## One clean sentence

[Compress the entire emotional state into a single sentence.]
```

---

## Rules for Your Lens

These are not suggestions. If a submission breaks these rules, it will be rejected.

1. **Stay inside the listener's experience.** You are describing what the music does to the person hearing it. Not what the songwriter meant. Not what the lyrics say literally.

2. **Describe, do not interpret.** "This feels like standing in an empty room" is a description. "This is about the artist's divorce" is an interpretation. One belongs here. The other doesn't.

3. **No external systems.** No politics, no social commentary, no cultural analysis, no moral frameworks. The lens doesn't care what the song is "about" in the world. It cares what the song does inside the listener.

4. **No moral judgment.** The lens doesn't decide if a song's content is good or bad, right or wrong, healthy or unhealthy.

5. **No advice.** The lens never tells the listener what to do with what they feel.

6. **Use the movement vocabulary.** Stillness, Build, Drift, Loop, Hold, Break, Return, Rise. You can combine them. You can chain them. Don't invent new ones unless they're clearly defined and necessary.

7. **Use the tone vocabulary.** Heavy, Light, Tense, Calm, Expansive, Detached, Warm, Cold. These describe the weight and temperature of the sound.

8. **Keep language human.** No academic jargon. No music theory lectures. Write like you're describing the feeling to someone sitting next to you. If a technical detail (time signature, key change, production technique) serves the emotional description, include it. If it doesn't, leave it out.

9. **The final output must feel like something experienced, not explained.** If your lens reads like a Wikipedia article or a music review, start over.

---

## Quality Check

Before submitting, ask yourself:

- Does this describe a feeling, or does it explain a meaning?
- Would someone who has never heard this song understand the emotional state?
- Would someone who knows this song by heart recognize what I'm describing?
- Does the "one clean sentence" land on its own, without the rest of the lens?
- Did I follow the template exactly?

If the answer to any of these is no, revise before submitting.

---

## What Gets Rejected

- Lyric-by-lyric breakdowns
- Artist biography or intent analysis
- Political or social commentary
- Moral conclusions about the song's content
- Submissions that don't follow the template
- Writing that sounds like a review instead of an experience

---

## What Gets Accepted

A lens that makes someone say: "Yeah. That's exactly what that song does."
