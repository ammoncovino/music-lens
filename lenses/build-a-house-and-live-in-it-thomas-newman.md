# Build a House and Live In It — Thomas Newman

**Track 11 / 2:07 / Passengers (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack), 2016**

---

## What this song feels like

Arriving somewhere soft. Not a destination — more like the moment you stop looking for one. There's warmth here, but it's careful warmth. An oboe melody moving over gentle strings and piano, and it feels the way deciding to stay feels. Quiet. Deliberate. A little tender in a way that could break if you pressed on it.

## Core emotional movement

**Build → Hold**

The piece gathers slowly — not from tension, but from openness. It layers warmth on warmth. Oboe over strings, piano underneath, each voice arriving gently as if choosing to be there. Then it holds. It doesn't climb toward anything larger. It just reaches its fullness and stays.

## What it's really about (emotionally)

The act of choosing to make something where you are, instead of waiting for where you were supposed to be. Not resignation. Not defeat. Something closer to the moment you stop grieving a plan and start building from what's real. The emotional state of choosing presence over longing.

## How the music mirrors it

Newman's oboe — his most personal voice — carries the melody like a single clear thought. The strings don't push it forward; they hold it in place. The piano anchors without weighing anything down. At just over two minutes, the piece doesn't overextend. It states itself simply. The orchestration is warm but not lush — there's still space in it, still air, like a room that has furniture now but isn't full yet. The track sits at the apex of the score's romantic second quarter, the moment of deepest emotional sincerity before things begin to shift.

## Emotional center

Commitment to where you are. Building not because everything is right, but because you're here.

## One clean sentence

The sound of someone deciding to live in the life they have instead of the one they imagined.
