# At Your Service — Steve Jablonsky

**Track / 3:10 / The Last Witch Hunter (Original Soundtrack Album), 2015**

---

## What this song feels like

Standing up after everything has been fought through. Not celebration — something quieter than that. There's weight behind this piece, but it isn't heavy anymore. It's the feeling of arriving at the far side of something brutal and still being upright. Brass and strings open wide, and the sensation is something like: duty met, presence confirmed.

## Core emotional movement

**Rise → Hold**

The track lifts from the dense, action-driven material that precedes it in the score — the chaos of "By Iron and Fire," the urgency and pressure of the battle cues. "At Your Service" doesn't explode upward. It rises with intention, then holds at its height. Not climbing endlessly. Just reaching full posture and staying there.

## What it's really about (emotionally)

The internal state of someone who has chosen to keep showing up. Not heroism as spectacle — heroism as posture. The feeling isn't triumph. It's closer to alignment: the self squared up with what it's been asked to carry. There's loyalty in it. Endurance. A willingness to continue that doesn't need to be loud.

## How the music mirrors it

Jablonsky writes in the orchestral blockbuster tradition — full brass, deep percussion, sweeping strings — but this cue pulls back from the relentless density of the preceding action. The tempo steadies. The brass doesn't charge; it announces. Strings provide warmth underneath rather than tension. At 3:10, the track has enough time to breathe into its shape without overstaying. It sits at the end of the suite as a resolution — not a finale with fireworks, but a conclusion that lands firmly on its feet.

## Emotional center

Readiness. Still here. Still standing. Still willing.

## One clean sentence

The sound of someone planting their feet after the storm and saying, without words, that they'll do it again.
