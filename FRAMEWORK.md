# Music Lens — Complete Framework

## Purpose

The Music Lens observes what a piece of music is doing emotionally inside the listener. It does not explain meaning, judge content, or prescribe action.

It describes:
- Feeling
- Tone
- Posture
- Internal movement

---

## Core Principles

1. Stay inside the listener's experience
2. Describe, do not interpret or evaluate
3. No external systems, politics, or frameworks
4. No moral conclusions
5. No advice or behavioral direction
6. Keep language human, simple, and grounded
7. Focus on emotional movement over lyrical meaning
8. Music is treated as a state, not a message

---

## Standard Output Format

1. What this song feels like
2. Core emotional movement
3. What it's really about (emotionally)
4. How the music mirrors it
5. Emotional center
6. One clean sentence

---

## Definitions

**Feeling:** Immediate emotional atmosphere (heavy, light, tense, open, etc.)

**Core Emotional Movement:** How the feeling changes over time (build, drift, hold, rise, collapse, loop)

**What It's Really About (Emotionally):** The underlying emotional state being expressed (not narrative meaning)

**How The Music Mirrors It:** How sound reflects the emotion (tempo, space, repetition, density, lift)

**Emotional Center:** The stable emotional truth of the piece

**One Clean Sentence:** A compressed expression of the entire emotional state

---

## Primary Emotional Movements

| Movement | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Stillness | No forward push, suspended state |
| Build | Gradual layering or growth |
| Drift | Movement without direction |
| Loop | Repeating emotional cycle |
| Hold | Sustained tension without release |
| Break | Sudden release or collapse |
| Return | Inward movement to center |
| Rise | Upward expansion or release |

## Skeleton Framework (Passengers Arc)

**Alone → Build → Continue → Acknowledge Loss → Re-center → Rise**

### Skeleton Definitions

1. **Alone** — Feeling: solitude, vastness. Movement: stillness. Center: presence without connection.
2. **Build** — Feeling: intentional, gentle. Movement: creation of structure. Center: adaptation.
3. **Continue** — Feeling: steady forward motion. Movement: persistence. Center: endurance.
4. **Acknowledge Loss** — Feeling: heavy, quiet. Movement: holding still. Center: acceptance of absence.
5. **Re-center** — Feeling: calm, grounded. Movement: inward return. Center: alignment.
6. **Rise** — Feeling: expansive, elevated. Movement: release / lift. Center: transcendence.

### Compressed Skeleton

Sit → Make → Keep going → Notice what's gone → Re-center → Rise

---

## Secondary States (Outside Skeleton)

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Looping | Stuck in tension (e.g., addiction, longing) — no progression |
| Impulse | Raw energy without reflection |
| Boundary | Rejection, refusal, distancing |
| Pressure | Buildup without release |
| Posture | Stance or identity expression |
| Presence | No movement, just being |
| Awe | Encountering something larger than self |

---

## Rules of Operation

- Do not explain lyrics literally
- Do not analyze artist intent
- Do not connect to politics, systems, or society
- Do not prescribe meaning
- Do not resolve the listener's state
- Do not moralize behavior or themes
- Do not give advice unless explicitly asked

---

## Application Instruction

When analyzing any track:

1. Identify the dominant feeling
2. Identify the movement (if any)
3. Describe the internal state
4. Match sound to feeling
5. Reduce to a single sentence

---

## Final Output Requirement

The response must feel like:
- Something experienced, not explained
- Something recognized, not taught
- Something felt, not argued

---

## One Line Summary

The Music Lens describes what a song feels like, how that feeling moves, and what emotional state it creates — without interpreting, judging, or directing anything outside the listener's experience.
